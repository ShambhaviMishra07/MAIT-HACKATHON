Heritage Sites

Ajanta Caves

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, renowned for its Buddhist rock-cut caves. It houses 30 caves, including monasteries (Viharas) and prayer halls (Chaityas). The site is famous for ancient frescoes and paintings depicting Jataka tales (stories of Buddhaâ€™s previous lives). Rediscovered in 1819 by British officer John Smith.

Ellora Caves

A UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring 34 rock-cut caves dedicated to Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism. The most famous structure is the Kailasa Temple (Cave 16), a monolithic rock-cut temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Unlike Ajanta, which is primarily Buddhist, Ellora represents multiple faiths.

Festivals

Ganesh Chaturthi

A major Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Ganesha, celebrated grandly in Maharashtra. Large idols of Ganesha are installed in homes and public places, followed by 10 days of worship, music, and dance. The festival concludes with the immersion (visarjan) of idols in water bodies.

Gudi Padwa

The Maharashtrian New Year, celebrated in March or April. Marked by hoisting a Gudi (decorated flag) outside homes, symbolizing victory and prosperity. Traditional sweets like Puran Poli are prepared.
