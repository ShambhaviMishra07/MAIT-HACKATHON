Delhi

Akshardham Temple

Swaminarayan Akshardham, inaugurated in 2005, is one of the largest Hindu temple complexes in the world. Built using pink sandstone and white marble, it showcases ancient Indian culture, spirituality, and architecture.

The temple features intricately carved sculptures, a mesmerizing water show, and an exhibition on Indian traditions.

Jama Masjid

Jama Masjid, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1656, is one of the largest mosques in India. It features three grand gates, four towers, and two towering minarets made of red sandstone and white marble.

The mosque can accommodate over 25,000 worshippers and offers a breathtaking view of Old Delhi from its minarets.

Holi

The festival of colors, celebrated in March. People play with colored powders (gulal), throw water balloons, and dance. Special sweets like gujiya and drinks like thandai are enjoyed.

Diwali

The festival of lights, celebrated across India in October or November. Homes and temples are decorated with diyas (oil lamps) and rangoli. Fireworks, sweets, and family gatherings are an essential part of the celebration.
