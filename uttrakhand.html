Uttarakhand

Heritage Sites

Kedarnath Temple

Located in the Himalayas, Kedarnath Temple is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. It is part of the Char Dham pilgrimage and is situated at an altitude of 3,583 meters. The temple was built by the Pandavas and later revived by Adi Shankaracharya. It remains open only from April to November due to extreme weather.

Badrinath Temple

Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, Badrinath Temple is another important part of the Char Dham pilgrimage. It is situated at 3,300 meters in Uttarakhand and is visited by thousands of devotees every year. The temple remains open from May to November. The sacred Alaknanda River flows near the temple.

Festivals

Kumbh Mela

Held in Haridwar every 12 years, the Kumbh Mela is one of the largest religious gatherings in the world. Devotees take a holy dip in the Ganges to cleanse their sins. It features sadhus, saints, and pilgrims from all over India. The last Kumbh Mela in Haridwar was in 2021.

Makar Sankranti (Ghughutia Festival)

Celebrated in January, it marks the transition of the Sun into Capricorn. In Uttarakhand, it is known as Ghughutia or Uttarayani. People eat sesame and jaggery sweets, and children wear garlands of sweets to attract birds.
